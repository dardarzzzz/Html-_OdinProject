JerkChicken Recipe

This spicy, smoky jerk chicken is bursting with Caribbean flavor. Chicken thighs are marinated in a bold blend of scotch bonnet peppers, allspice, and thyme, then grilled until charred and juicy. Serve it with rice and peas for an authentic island meal.


Ingredients:

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs

  • 3 green onions, chopped

  • 1 scotch bonnet or habanero pepper, seeded

  • 1 small onion, chopped

  • 3 cloves garlic

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce

  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar

  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves

  • 2 teaspoons ground allspice

  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

  • 1 lime, juiced

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

  • Salt and pepper to taste


Directions:

  1. Combine green onions, scotch bonnet pepper, onion, garlic, soy sauce, brown sugar, thyme, allspice, cinnamon, lime juice, oil, salt, and pepper in a blender. Blend until smooth to make the jerk marinade.

  2. Place chicken thighs in a large bowl or resealable bag. Pour marinade over the chicken, coating all pieces well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

  3. Preheat a grill to medium-high heat. Remove chicken from marinade, shaking off excess.

  4. Grill chicken, turning occasionally, until the skin is charred and the internal temperature reaches 165°F, about 30-35 minutes. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving.
